Here is the required PREA response, broken down into critical steps.

### **Immediate and Mandatory Actions (First 24 Hours)**

The moment an allegation is received (whether through a report, grievance, or other means), the following actions are legally required:

**1. Protect the Youth (Victim/Survivor):**
*   **Immediate Safety:** The youth must be immediately separated from the accused staff member. Under no circumstances should they have any further contact.
*   **Safe Housing:** The youth should be moved to a safe and supportive environment within the facility. This should not feel like punitive segregation. Their safety and emotional state are the paramount concerns.
*   **Preservation of Evidence:** If the allegation is recent, the youth should be offered a forensic medical examination (rape kit) performed by a specially trained professional, such as a Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner (SANE). This examination must be offered **even if the youth appears physically unharmed**, as it can preserve critical evidence. The youth has the right to decline this examination.
*   **Preservation of Crime Scene:** If applicable, the area where the alleged incident occurred should be secured to preserve any physical evidence.

**2. Report the Allegation:**
*   **To the Facility Administrator:** The warden or director must be notified immediately.
*   **To Law Enforcement:** **PREA is a CRIME.** The allegation must be reported immediately to the appropriate law enforcement agency with jurisdiction (e.g., state police, local sheriff). This is non-negotiable. The facility cannot conduct an internal investigation in lieu of a criminal investigation.
*   **To Child Protective Services (CPS):** Because the victim is a minor in a custodial setting, the allegation must also be reported to CPS or the relevant state child welfare agency, as per mandatory reporting laws.

**3. Secure Evidence and Documentation:**
*   Document everything: who reported the allegation, the time, the date, and the exact details provided.
*   Ensure any relevant video footage, logs, or other potential evidence is preserved and not recorded over.

### **Ongoing Required Actions (Following the Immediate Response)**

**4. Provide Victim Support Services:**
*   **Access to Advocates:** The youth must be provided with immediate and ongoing access to victim advocacy services. This includes emotional support, crisis counseling, and information about their rights.
*   **Medical and Mental Health Care:** The youth must be offered comprehensive medical and mental health care, including trauma-informed therapy, at no cost to them or their family.
*   **Confidentiality:** The youth's privacy must be protected to the greatest extent possible, consistent with the need to conduct the investigation.

**5. Manage the Accused Staff Member:**
*   **Administrative Leave:** The accused staff member must be placed on paid administrative leave immediately. This is a standard procedure to ensure the integrity of the investigation, prevent any potential intimidation of the youth or witnesses, and ensure staff safety. It is not a presumption of guilt.
*   **Restrict Access:** The staff member's access to the facility and all residents must be revoked.

**6. Initiate the Investigations:**
PREA requires a **dual-track investigation**:
*   **Criminal Investigation:** Conducted by the external law enforcement agency. This investigation determines if criminal charges will be filed.
*   **Administrative Investigation:** Conducted by the agency's internal affairs unit or an external third-party investigator. This investigation determines if agency policies (like PREA) were violated and what administrative actions (e.g., termination, decertification) should be taken.

**7. Notify the Public and Agencies (As Required):**
*   The facility or agency is required to make public certain aggregated data about PREA incidents in its annual reports.
*   The agency may also have a duty to notify other specific entities, such as oversight bodies, depending on state law and agency policy.

### **Key PREA Standards to Remember**

*   **No Retaliation:** It is illegal to retaliate against the youth, any other residents, or staff who report the allegation or cooperate with the investigation. The facility must actively monitor for and prevent retaliation.
*   **Staff Training:** All staff must be trained on PREA standards, including their mandatory reporting obligations and how to recognize and respond to sexual misconduct.
*   **Youth Education:** Youth in custody must be educated about their right to be safe from sexual abuse and the various ways they can report abuse (verbally, in writing, to a third party, etc.).
*   **Audits:** Facilities are subject to regular, independent PREA audits to ensure compliance with all these standards.
